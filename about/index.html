My Background

Growing up, I was often surrounded by people who love the art of photography. They, especially my dad and grandpa, taught me how to find beauty and balance in the smallest details of a setting, and how to turn that beauty into a photograph that could be preserved. As I have grown up, I gained a love for two types of photography in particular: portrait and black & white photography.

Portrait and B&W Photography

Although they exist as two fairly different facets of photography, one being a genre and the other being a style, portrait and black and white photography have held my attention for most of my photography career. I love to work with both movement and light in my shoots. Photographing people and animals provides the natural movement needed for a candid, grounded photo. Working in contrasting styles like black and white forces you to focus on the lighting of your surroundings and think about how you can best use it to produce beauty even from a seemingly plain and boring canvas.
